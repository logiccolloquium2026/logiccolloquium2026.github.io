Speaker: Philipp Hieronymi

Title

Logic, Geometry, and Computation: A Survey of expansions of additive structures

Abstract

We survey recent results on expansions of Presburger arithmetic and of the ordered additive group of the reals. Such structures have long been studied by logicians and theoretical computer scientists for a variety of reasons. In this survey, we provide an overview of recent developments in the area and explain their relevance to model theory, theoretical computer science, and tame geometry.

This series of talks aims partially update and connect the following earlier surveys: "A Survey of Arithmetical Definability" by Alexis Bes, "Open questions around Büchi and Presburger arithmetics" by Christian Michaux and Roger Villemaire and "Tameness in expansions of the real field" by Chris Miller.
